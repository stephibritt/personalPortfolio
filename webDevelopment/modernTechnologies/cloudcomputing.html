Using Data in the Cloud

What is Cloud Computing?

Isn't that just for backups?

  • Cloud Computing is not only used for the storage of personal photographs and videos through the Internet, but many different computing services.
  • Cloud Computing focuses on providing storage and computing hardware and software use from a remote site and is provided over the Internet or by an extranet* with the user's own systems.
  • Cloud Computing can include services such as data backup, Software-as-a-Service and Infrastructure-as-a-Service.
  • Many large-scale companies use Cloud Computing services to enable their operations to quickly grow as their business needs grow.

How does Cloud Computing Work?

What can it provide?

  • Users establish a connection with a service provider capable of hosting the needed type of service.
  • The service provider is responsible for providing the hardware and software needs for the service being offered, such as extensive storage space, faster operating computer systems, or specialized software.
  • The user contracts for the amount of these services that they need, which can be modified as the user's needs change.
  • The user does not have to deal with the cost of purchasing new hardware and software or the maintenance on these systems.

What is Cloud Computing's Future?

What is its impact?

  • By providing computing services and storage remotely, Cloud Computing companies can incorporate economies of scale to reduce the costs of providing their services.
  • Companies can also grow and provide greater services to their own clients without having to worry about the costs involved to increase their computational capacity.
  • Cloud Computing allows for users from around the world to have access to the same level of technology and functionality regardless of whether the technology is in their home location.
  • As Cloud Computing grows, improvements in technology can be more quickly shared and incorporated into mainstream usage.

The Future of Cloud Computing

One of the more likely developments in the Cloud Computing world might just be its adoption among non-commercial users. Individuals might one day choose to "rent" computing time and power from a centralized cloud server, running all their operations remotely through their smartphones or their tablet devices.
