Glossary and References

Definitions

Digital Assets
Digital assets are electronically-created forms of information that have a definite value.
Examples include: NFTs, Etherium, BitCoin.
Distributed Databases
Distributed systems, including distributed databases, are systems that are not located on one central computer or even one network. Instead, they are spread across multiple computers across different locations.
Distributed systems can be owned by one entity, or may be the shared computing power of computers owned by many different users across the world.
Hashed Values
A hashed value is data that has been scrambled in a complex, algorithmic way to make identifying or changing the source data extremely difficult.
Hashing is frequently used for securing passwords and private data. In the blockchain, the information of each element of the chain is hashed together to generate a link used to unique identify the next block in the chain.
Extranet
An Extranet is a network structure that connects a company's computer network to the trusted networks of other companies or to individuals that the company trusts.
Extranet connections may be through a Virtual Private Network, over a secured Internet connection, or by direct physical connections.
