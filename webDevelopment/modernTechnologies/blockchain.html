The Power of the Blockchain

What is the Blockchain

That's BitCoin, right?

  • The blockchain is a data structure that allows for a secure record of ownership of digital assets*.
  • The blockchain is established within a distributed system of computers that house multiple ledgers of information used to assign ownership.
  • The blockchain allows for multiple ledgers to update their information in synchronization with their peers to maintain an accurate chronological record of all transactions.
  • In addition to storing the connections between each ledger entry, the blockchain directly links with the digital asset that it represents and secures.

How Does the Blockchain Work

Is it really secure?

  • The blockchain works by allowing widely distributed databases* across the world to host the blockchain database structures.
  • These blockchain structures use a series of hashed* values built from the data that makes up previous entries starting with the first time the item was created to securely link each transaction.
  • By linking the list of transactions in a hashed and secured way, the blockchain makes it impossible to change an entry without first removing all entries that follow after it.
  • The distributed blockchain structures also share their changes quickly throughout the distributed network, while also checking for discrepancies and validating transactions.

How Blockchain will impact the Future

What can blockchain become?

  • As more technology develops, the need to secure new types of digital assets, such as NFTs and Cryptocurrencies will expand the need for blockchain to maintain the owner's rights.
  • Because the blockchain is not limited to a single computer system or network, but instead can be spread globally through distributed systems, it is not impacted by changes in politics or the policies of individual countries.
  • In the Metaverse, one of its most important features will be the ability to own a digital asset across platforms. This means that ownership is exclusive regardless of where it is.
  • Although it may not be perfect now, developments in how blockchains work will continue to provide the strongest security possible.
